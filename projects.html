üìä Manufacturing Digital Twin

Orizon Aerostructures | Summer 2024

Project Overview

Digital twins are transforming modern manufacturing, and I experienced this transformation firsthand at Orizon Aerostructures, a Tier 1 aerospace manufacturer producing 75% of F-35 components and wingboxes for Boeing aircraft. Recognizing critical inefficiencies in production visibility across our manufacturing floor, I researched digital twin technology and designed a comprehensive implementation strategy to bridge the gap between our physical operations and digital systems.

The Challenge

Our manufacturing floor faced a fundamental problem: lack of real-time visibility into part location, production status, and quality metrics. This created several cascading issues:

  • Delayed delivery forecasts due to manual part tracking
  • Limited visibility into production bottlenecks
  • Reactive (rather than predictive) quality control
  • Disconnected data between ERP systems and shop floor reality

I identified that real-time data capture was the critical missing piece preventing us from leveraging digital twin technology.

My Solution

I designed a multi-layered digital twin implementation strategy combining IoT sensors, computer vision, and data integration:

üé• Vision-Based Part Tracking

Deployed camera-equipped Raspberry Pi systems at each manufacturing station to scan QR codes on parts, enabling real-time location tracking and accurate delivery forecasting.

üîß Smart Tooling Integration

Proposed sensor-equipped tooling (smart drills with torque and position monitoring) to capture quality metrics in real-time, creating a foundation for predictive quality control.

üìä Data Pipeline Architecture

Built SQL ‚Üí Power BI pipeline integrating ERP and floor data, transforming raw manufacturing data into actionable real-time insights.

üéØ Continuous Improvement Loop

Established feedback mechanisms enabling data-driven process optimization and predictive maintenance scheduling.

Impact & Results

Through this implementation, I witnessed firsthand how digital twins can transform manufacturing operations:

  • Reduced rework through real-time quality monitoring and early defect detection
  • Optimized throughput by identifying and eliminating production bottlenecks
  • Enabled data-driven decision-making across production planning and resource allocation
  • Improved delivery accuracy with real-time part tracking and forecasting

Technologies Used

Power BI SQL Raspberry Pi Python IoT Sensors Computer Vision ERP Integration Real-Time Data Processing

Key Learnings

This project solidified my understanding that digital twins aren't just about technology‚Äîthey're about fundamentally changing how manufacturing operations work. The key to successful implementation is:

  • Starting with real problems rather than technology solutions
  • Building incremental capabilities that deliver immediate value
  • Ensuring data quality and accessibility at every layer
  • Creating actionable insights that drive behavioral change

This experience showed me the transformative potential of digital twins and reinforced my commitment to building the next generation of industrial operations platforms.
